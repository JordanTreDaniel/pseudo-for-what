Why do we Pseudo-Code?

  • Understanding: If you can't explain something simply, you don't understand it. You should understand your programs.
  • Efficiency: Not planning our code leads to:
    1. Poorly Engineered, what-the-h***-is-this looking-a** code.
    2. Wasted time. It seems slower to do it at first, but this habit pays you in precious time.
    3. Frustration and despair. You can avoid them both!
  • The Jobs!! If employers ask you to write an algorithm, and you don't think it through first, you won't get the job.
  • More Jobs!! Many interviews simply consist of whiteboarding, which is basically pseudo-coding.
